LunaShot helps you calculate the perfect moment when the moon aligns with a landmark.
Plan your "Full Moon over the Tower" shot with precision.

ğŸš€ How to Use

1. Select Date Pick a date from the top date picker. Tonight or the next Full Moon is a great start.
2. Set Target Click on the map to select your subject (building, mountain peak). A red marker will appear.
* Use the search bar to find locations easily.
3. Enter Height Enter the height of the object in meters.
  • Moonrise: If you just want to see the moon rising from the horizon, leave it at 0.
  • Moonset: Height is crucial if you want to capture the moon setting on top of a building.
  • Examples: N Seoul Tower (~236m), Lotte World Tower (~555m)
4. Draw Lines Click the button.
Blue lines (Moonrise) and Orange lines (Moonset) will appear on the map.
5. Find Shooter Location Click anywhere on or near the lines to place your shooting spot (Blue marker).
6. Check Results Calculation results will appear at the bottom.
  • ğŸ“¸ Shot Window: Start and end time of the alignment.
  • Red Solid Line: Moon direction at start time.
  • Red Dashed Line: Moon direction at end time.
Adjust your location so the target fits within the red lines.

ğŸ’¡ Tips

  • Distance: The further you are from the target, the larger the moon appears relative to the building.
